This document provides an outline of key terms to discuss with:

### Section 3: Vesting Schedule

**Why Vesting Matters:**
Vesting protects both partners if someone leaves early. Without vesting, a partner could leave after 3 months and retain full ownership.

**Key Questions:**

| Question | Options | Your Decision |
|----------|---------|---------------|
| Will equity be subject to vesting? | Yes (recommended) / No | |
| What is the vesting period? | 3 years, 4 years (standard) | |
| Is there a cliff? | 1 year cliff (standard) | |
| What vesting schedule? | Monthly, quarterly, annually | |
| Acceleration on exit/sale? | Single trigger, double trigger, none | |

**Standard 4-Year Vesting with 1-Year Cliff:**

```
Year 1: 0% vested until 12 months, then 25% vests at cliff
Year 2: Additional 25% vests (monthly or quarterly)
Year 3: Additional 25% vests
Year 4: Additional 25% vests → 100% fully vested
```

**Example:**

- Partner has 40% ownership with 4-year vesting, 1-year cliff
- Leaves after 6 months → forfeits all 40%
- Leaves after 18 months → keeps 25% + 6 months of vesting = ~31.25%
- Stays 4 years → keeps full 40%

**Discussion Points:**

- Should vesting apply to both partners equally?
- What triggers acceleration (company sale, termination without cause)?
- Should there be credit for work done before formal agreement?

### Section 7: Decision-Making & Governance

**Decision Categories:**

| Category | Examples | Approval Required |
|----------|----------|-------------------|
| Day-to-day operations | Vendor selection, minor expenses | Managing Member alone |
| Significant decisions | Hiring, major contracts, pricing changes | Majority vote |
| Major decisions | Taking investment, selling company, new partners | Unanimous consent |

**Proposed Thresholds:**

| Decision Type | Threshold | Approval |
|---------------|-----------|----------|
| Expenses under $X | $500 | No approval needed |
| Expenses $X - $Y | $500 - $5,000 | Managing Member |
| Expenses over $Y | $5,000+ | Both partners |
| Contracts under $X/year | $10,000 | Managing Member |
| Contracts over $X/year | $10,000+ | Both partners |
| Hiring employees | Any | Both partners |
| Taking on debt | Any | Both partners |
| Equity transactions | Any | Unanimous |

**Deadlock Resolution:**

| Method | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Mediation | Neutral third party facilitates agreement |
| Buy-sell (shotgun) | One partner offers to buy or sell at set price |
| Casting vote | Designated partner breaks ties (usually CEO) |
| Dissolution | If irreconcilable, company winds down |

**Discussion Points:**

- Who is the "Managing Member" with day-to-day authority?
- What decisions require unanimous consent?
- How do you resolve disagreements?

### Section 11: Departure & Exit Provisions

**Departure Scenarios:**

| Scenario | Equity Treatment | Other Considerations |
|----------|------------------|----------------------|
| Voluntary resignation | Unvested forfeited, vested retained (or buyback) | Non-compete applies |
| Termination for cause | All equity forfeited | Define "cause" clearly |
| Termination without cause | Vested retained, possible acceleration | Severance? |
| Death | Vested to estate, buyback option | Life insurance? |
| Disability | Similar to death | Define disability |
| Partner buyout | Fair market value or formula | Payment terms |

**Buyback Rights:**

| Provision | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Company right to repurchase | On any departure |
| Valuation method | Fair market value, formula, or negotiated |
| Payment terms | Lump sum, installments (12-24 months) |
| Departing partner cooperation | Required for transition |

**Valuation Methods:**

| Method | Description | Pros | Cons |
|--------|-------------|------|------|
| Third-party appraisal | Independent valuation | Objective | Expensive |
| Formula-based | Revenue multiple, book value | Simple, predictable | May not reflect true value |
| Negotiated | Partners agree | Flexible | Can lead to disputes |
| Last round price | Most recent funding valuation | Simple | May be outdated |

**Discussion Points:**

- What happens to equity if a partner leaves in year 2?
- Can/should the company buy back vested equity?
- How do you value the company if there's no recent funding?
- What's "cause" for termination?

# Part 2: Advisor Agreement

## For: Physician Advisor

---

### Section 1: Advisor Role Overview

**Purpose of Advisor Relationship:**

- Clinical guidance on target markets and drug selection
- Credibility with potential customers (physicians)
- Potential customer introductions
- Industry insight and strategic advice

**This is NOT:**

- A full-time position
- An employment relationship
- A guarantee of compensation beyond equity

---

### Section 2: Advisor Responsibilities

**Proposed Advisor Duties:**

| Duty | Expected Time |
|------|---------------|
| Monthly advisory calls | 1-2 hours/month |
| Email availability for questions | As needed |
| Customer introductions | When appropriate |
| Review of clinical approach | Quarterly |
| Attendance at key meetings | 2-4 per year |
| Reference/credibility | Ongoing |

**What the Advisor is NOT Responsible For:**

- Day-to-day operations
- PA review or clinical validation work
- Sales or business development
- Financial decisions
- Legal or compliance (beyond clinical input)

---

### Section 3: Advisor Compensation

**Standard Advisor Equity:**

| Stage | Typical Equity Range | Notes |
|-------|---------------------|-------|
| Pre-seed/Idea stage | 0.25% - 1.0% | Higher risk, more equity |
| Seed stage | 0.1% - 0.5% | Moderate risk |
| Series A+ | 0.05% - 0.25% | Lower risk, less equity |

**Recommended for FirstPass Rx:**

- Stage: Pre-seed / Idea
- Range: 0.25% - 0.5%
- Vesting: 2 years, monthly, no cliff (or 1-year cliff)

**Vesting Schedule Options:**

| Option | Description | Best For |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| 2-year monthly | Standard advisor vesting | Most situations |
| 1-year with cliff | 100% after 1 year | Low-commitment advisors |
| Milestone-based | Vests upon specific achievements | Intro-focused advisors |

**Cash Compensation:**

- Typically none for equity advisors
- Expense reimbursement only
- Some companies offer small honorarium ($500-2K/year)

| Provision | Typical Terms |
|-----------|---------------|
| Initial term | 1-2 years |
| Renewal | Mutual agreement |
| Termination | Either party, 30 days notice |
| Vesting on termination | Vested equity retained |

**Advisor Confidentiality Obligations:**

- [ ] Keep business information confidential
- [ ] Not disclose to third parties
- [ ] Not use for personal benefit
- [ ] Return materials upon termination
- [ ] Survives termination

### Section 6: Other Advisor Terms

**Standard Provisions:**

| Provision | Notes |
|-----------|-------|
| No employment relationship | Advisor is independent |

# Part 3: Key Discussion Questions Summary

## Questions for Pharmacist Partner

### Commitment & Vision

1. What is your long-term vision for involvement—part-time forever, or potentially full-time?
2. How many hours per week can you realistically commit during the pilot? During growth?
3. Are you comfortable with $0 salary until we hit [revenue milestone]?
4. What would make you walk away from this venture?

### Current Situation

1. Do you have any existing non-compete or non-disclosure agreements from current/past employers?
2. Are you comfortable with a vesting schedule on your equity?
3. What cash contribution, if any, can you make to startup costs?

### Working Together

1. How should we make decisions when we disagree?
2. What does success look like to you in 1 year? 3 years? 5 years?
3. How do you prefer to communicate (calls, texts, Slack)?

### Exit

1. What happens if you need to step back due to life circumstances?
2. Are you comfortable with a buyback provision on your equity?
3. Would you be open to bringing in a third partner if needed?

---

## Questions for Physician Advisor

### Value & Contribution

1. What unique value can you bring beyond credibility (introductions, insights)?
2. How many customer introductions do you think you could realistically provide?
3. Are you connected to specialty practices that struggle with PAs?

### Commitment

1. How much time can you realistically dedicate (monthly)?
2. Are you comfortable with equity-only compensation?
3. How long are you willing to commit as an advisor?

### Conflicts

1. Do you have any relationships with PBMs, pharma companies, or competitors?
2. Does your employer have any policies about outside advisory roles?
3. Are you advising any other healthcare startups?
